Course platform & timetable

We are using an online course platform this year. The platform hosts the live timetable, candidate assessment tools, session guides, and messaging. This section explains how it works so you are familiar with it before the course.

Your timetable links — save these to your phone's home screen before the course:

Live timetable. The timetable is live throughout the course. A highlighted banner shows which session is happening at any given moment. If timings change, the timetable updates immediately — you will always have the most accurate version. Candidates see a simplified version of the same timetable showing only their sessions.

Session guides. Each session in the timetable has an expandable detail section. Where applicable, this includes a link to the session guide — a step-by-step reference for running that session. These are accessible directly from the timetable row.

Candidate assessment. Your faculty toolbar (visible at the bottom of the screen when signed in) gives you direct access to the BLS Gateway assessment, the Development assessment form, and the Assessment Dashboard. These replace the Google Forms used in previous years. All assessments are stored in real time and visible to the course director.

Teaching approach
⚠ Important — 2025 RCUK Guidelines & Teaching Approach
We are teaching to the 2025 RCUK Resuscitation Guidelines this year. These contain differences in the BLS algorithm compared to what we have been teaching previously. Please review the updated guidelines before the course to ensure you are familiar with the changes. The full guidelines are at resus.org.uk/professional-library/2025-resuscitation-guidelines.

In line with these updated guidelines, we are reverting to the four-stage approach for skills teaching on this course.

Our teaching approach aligns with the current RCUK guidelines.

Note: We do not use the Set – dialogue – closure approach on this course.

The stepwise teaching approach:

Show it Demonstrate the skill in real time without added narrative explanation.
Teach it Teach the concept and the course standard.
Facilitate it Get all candidates involved in trying the skill.
Assess it Confirm the course standard has been reached. This may be possible within the facilitation stage.

Encourage peer-to-peer learning where reasonable, drawing on candidates' previous experiences.

Session structure: Prepare – Open – Facilitate – Close
Prepare The instructor, having prepared the session content, room layout, and environment beforehand, also ensures all equipment is working, briefs any co-instructor on their role, and remains constantly aware of the time.
Open The instructor introduces all participants (self, co-instructor, and candidates), then outlines the session's flow, and clearly states the learning and assessment outcomes.
Facilitate The instructor first explains and demonstrates the skill, then facilitates candidate practice while acknowledging prior experience, assesses whether learning outcomes are met, provides or organises additional coaching as needed, and as necessary interacts with the co-instructor to confirm assessment decisions.
Close The instructor gains feedback from candidates, summarises key learning points, informs candidates of their successful or unsuccessful outcome, completes all necessary documentation, outlines next steps or future sessions, thanks the co-instructor and candidates, and ensures the area is left tidy.
The session guides, assessment tools, and slides have all been updated to reflect these approaches. Links to each session guide are embedded in the timetable.
